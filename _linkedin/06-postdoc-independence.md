🌱 Nobody warns you about the in-between.

As a postdoc, you're no longer a student, but you're not yet a PI. You're expected to lead, but most of the leading happens with no title, no team, and no one watching.

The work that actually builds independence is quiet. Defining your own questions instead of inheriting them. Saying no to projects that aren't yours. Spending months head-down on something that might not work, with nobody to tell you you're on the right track.

It rarely looks like progress from the outside. There are stretches where I don't post, don't publish, don't announce anything, just slow, unglamorous building.

For a long time that silence felt like falling behind. I'm learning to read it differently: it's the sound of a research line taking shape.

If you're in that in-between phase too, leading without the title, building something still invisible, you're not behind. You're doing the part that doesn't photograph well but matters most.

Independence isn't a moment you're granted. It's a thousand quiet choices no one sees, until one day the work is unmistakably yours. 🌿

To the other postdocs in the trenches: keep building. Let's connect.
